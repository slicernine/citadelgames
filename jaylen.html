Awards & Accolades

Unqualified for the position of Commander of Flagship Operations, special dispensation was granted that you be made a Chief Warrant Officer, and this position was created for you to occupy. Because of this, you have now met two intriguing individuals who have great potential to benefit this Flotilla: Adrian Rook, a cybertech slicer; and Tendra Dyala, a dedicated mechanic and former nurse.

  • Rank Gained, The Flotilla: C.W.O/
  • War Asset: Adrian Rook
  • War Asset: Tendra Dyala

Having passed the Officer's Exam in a high percentile, you have been commissioned at the rank of Lieutenant Commander in the Flotilla, and within the ranks of the Empire.

You passed your command exam and were given one of the Flotilla's fast attack wings.

After raising the spirits of the Suun Salla's crew during its retrofit, you've been made an honorary officer of the Suun Salla. The bridge crew seeks your company frequently, and you have a bunk aboard ship should you wish to take advantage of it. While aboard during an engagement, you grant the Suun Salla +5 EMS.

Darth Ares has dubbed you the first new Vanguard since his promotion. You operate within imperial military structure for the most part; however, you are now under Darth Ares' personal supervision, and his commands supersede any military orders.

An honor only Lord Malgus can bestow, this award signifies your importance to the Flotilla, and lets anyone who sees it know that you have the respect of the Master of the Odyssey himself.

  • +10 Flotilla Reputation (maxed)
  • Flotilla Reputation cannot fall below 75.
  • Add b to all friendly social checks against Flotilla Members.

Aesthetic Note: Your badges and bars are now red-backed, and your uniforms and armor have a red metallic pauldron on the right shoulder.

Completed Missions / Personal Archive

With the help of Tenth-Code, Commander Borrington, Commander Bader, and Sharayda, you discovered The Controller, the long-dead being who built The Spine. It appears he was responsible for the mechanical anomalies plaguing the Flotilla. Since your meeting, these blips have ceased.

Having barely passed the written examination for your previous position as chief mechanic of The Conquest, you discovered that the Sith Lords and the Proctor had secretly been surveilling you for command aptitude. After passing that test, you were given an option of four different positions, and selected the one best suited to fast-paced battle assignments: commanding officer of The Warhawk and the rest of its attack craft squadron.

Darth Ares called you aboard The Titan, and the two of you had it out. Attempting to goad you into self-revelation, Ares insulted and dismissed your contributions to the Flotilla, and you left his presence dejected, but determined to earn whatever place you had not thus far.
Unbeknownst to you, Ares went on to sponsor you for a command position in the Flotilla, resulting in your promotion. You now understand his test of your character: you had to face your own jealousy, and reject it. He made you truly doubt all you'd accomplished in the Flotilla, and forced you to show your drive to prove yourself, taking charge and maintaining your zealous loyalty to the Flotilla in face of this insult.
Passing his test with great gusto, you have earned Lord Ares' respect, and he named you Vanguard Caesar. You now stand as one of the Flotilla's elite defenders.

Having passed the officer's exam and the subsequent command entrance exam, you are now a commander in the Imperial Navy. As your career continues, you still have opportunity to earn promotions and special assignments, but your legacy amongst the celebrated Top Brass seems assured.

Upon constructing a new body, the former Controller has passed his title on to Tenth-Code. However, he wrote a failsafe into the Spine's core code that prevents its assets from working toward recognizable bias. Therefore, Tenth-Code must operate the Spine impartially, effectively immortalizing the distrust of your goals of conquest, and insuring the alliance between the Spine and the Sith will remain fairly distant.
